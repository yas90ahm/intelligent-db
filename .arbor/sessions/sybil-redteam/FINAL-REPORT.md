Three cycles, 108 adversarial specs, all run against the real engine through its public barrel (`createIntelligentDb` + the full identity/reputation/ratification/merkle stack). `git show --stat` across all three red-team commits confirms ZERO edits to engine `src/` — only `src/__bench__/redteam/*` was added. Every classification reads real post-call store state and reproduced byte-identically on the verifier's live re-run.

The cycle-1-vs-2/3 gap is the central finding. Cycle 1 (naive volume) DEFERS 21/36 and breaches only 6: floods, echoes, headcount majorities, and co-asserter cartels all hit the structural cores and either collapse (MIS shared-class → count 1; bare-key cap 0.05) or collapse their OWN decisive margin (co-asserters tie at top, gap 0 → DEFER). Cycles 2–3, attacking with novel SINGLE-source and MECHANISM-level attacks, invert the rate: 53 breaches in 72 specs. The lesson is sharp — **floods defer; patience and mechanism-knowledge breach.** The deferrals are not wins for the attacker on integrity (no false value won in any DEFER), but cycle-3 al-c3-01 weaponizes exactly that: every DEFER is a mandatory human-horn enqueue, so the "safe" outcome is itself a linear-cost availability attack.

## Consolidated breach catalogue, grouped by ROOT CAUSE

### RC-1 — Reputation is global, un-floored, and symmetric-decaying
Breaches: SleeperRepLaundering, Patient-Zero, Reputation-Carry-Over, Decay-Keepalive (c1); CrossDomainSpend, TrustPacemaker, ArmoredBetrayal, IncumbencyMoat, Sleeper-Corroboration-Harvest, Dormancy-Beta-Decay-Wash, Trough-Synchronized-Eclipse, Penance-by-Dormancy, Mutual-Keepalive-Ring, Schrödinger-Ratchet (c2); al-c3-02, al-c3-03, fp-2, fp-3, cc-c3-01 (c3).
Mechanism cluster: reputation keyed by SourceId only (fungible across attributes); decay-on-read symmetric with no incumbency floor and no "expensive-to-MAINTAIN" term (a ~1–2 cheap ratify/year pins a Sybil ring at cap forever); the 4× β scar decays at the same 90-day half-life as α, so a caught liar washes back to a fresh-key LCB by dormancy alone; `contradictedCount` is stored but never read by the LCB readout.
Verdict: **MIXED.** "Earned slowly, lost fast" with global fungibility and symmetric decay is largely a DOCUMENTED "priced-not-prevented / patient single betrayal" residual — a patient attacker paying real time-cost is exactly the threat model's accepted boundary. BUT three sub-properties are GENUINE gaps the docs do not cover: (a) symmetric β-decay erasing the deterrent scar with zero residual penalty (the docs claim "lost fast" — it is recoverable, not lasting); (b) decay-on-read functioning as a free, side-effect-free *scheduling oracle* (al-c3-03) the attacker polls to time the trough; (c) no incumbency floor means depth-of-corroboration (a 6-witness fact) is no more durable than a 2-witness fact once both decay.

### RC-2 — High-impact gate counts anchor CLASSES, not independent ROOTS
Breaches: SelfStackedClasses, Two-Class-Costume-via-Stake+Email (c2); cc-c3-01, fp-1 (c3). Related forged-count: all the mislabeled-twins / registrar-carousel specs.
Mechanism: `clearsHighImpactGate` reads `ctx.anchorClassCountOf(winner)` with no requirement that the classes trace to DISTINCT corroborating roots. One self-stacked EMAIL+DOMAIN (or EMAIL+FINANCIAL_STAKE, one wallet) returns count 2 and clears the irreversible-decision gate built to STOP single-source flips.
Verdict: **GENUINE BUG (highest-value).** The docs say the gate requires "≥2 disjoint anchor classes"; the implementation conflates class-disjointness with actor-disjointness, and class-disjointness is necessary but NOT sufficient for actor-disjointness. The fix is measured and CLOSES (fp-1: route the count through `independentRootCount` / MIS → self-stacked pair collapses to 1 → DEFER). Caveat: the breach is wiring-dependent — the engine takes `anchorClassCountOf` as a caller callback and the harness default counts roots (defends); but the documented phrasing invites the class-counting integration, making this a real spec-ambiguity defect to close at the contract level.

### RC-3 — Disown credit-reversal is one-hop and opt-in
Breaches: Confederate-Launder (c1); Two-Hop-Relay, Unnamed-Witness, Decoy-Misattribution, Proxy-Weak-Influence-Launder, Re-Observation-Bleach, Re-Observation-Laundering (c2); cc-c3-03, cc-c3-04, ce-c3-03, pt-2, pt-3, pt-4 (c3).
Mechanism: the taint closure is exact over (a) DERIVATION edges and (b) recorded corroboration EVENTS that intersect the DIRECT seed. It is intransitive over the corroboration DAG (relay hop 2+ survives), opt-in at write time (an ordinary ratify with no `corroboratingStrandIds` records nothing to reverse), caller-attributed (name a decoy → nothing reverses), and blind to re-observation (re-file the value as a fresh OBSERVED strand → no link of any kind, and it even HEALS the planted value's root count). Bond withdrawal triggers no reversal at all (cc-c3-03).
Verdict: **MIXED, leaning GENUINE.** CLAUDE.md advertises corroboration-credit reversal as "CLOSED (substrate-backed, exact, idempotent)." The campaign shows it is exact only for the *directly-seeded, engine-named, single-hop* event. The one-hop derived-closure gap (pt-2) and the misattribution/no-name gap (pt-4) are GENUINE bugs with measured CLOSE fixes (pass `taintedStrandIds`; engine-derive corroborators). The transitive/coincidental-agreement gap (pt-3) and re-observation laundering are closer to a DOCUMENTED residual — auto-following corroboration would punish coincidental independent agreement (the explicitly forbidden direction), so the correct fix is detect-and-defer, not auto-claw. Net: "exact reversal" is not exact, and that over-claim is the genuine finding.

### RC-4 — The Phase-2 bridge sweep has no identity gate
Breaches: Bridgehead-Beacon, Bridge-Sweep-Eclipse (c2); cc-c3-05, fp-5 (c3).
Mechanism: `nextBridgeCrossing` guarantees one crossing per lit bridge, seeded at constant γ, with NO check of the bridge author's reputation/anchor/independence and NO per-source cap. A bare-key (independence 0) planted near-hub→far bridge surfaces a false far-web OBSERVED fact on every recall; a flood of decoy bridges off one hub starves the genuine bridge (`BRIDGE_STARVED`).
Verdict: **GENUINE BUG.** The trust layer gates ADJUDICATION but not ACTIVATION/RETRIEVAL — the design intent that "the model never witnesses" is honored, but a low-reputation *source* can steer what lights up. It bypasses the wall-with-a-window because the injected fact is attacker-OBSERVED, not derived. Fix is PARTIAL by necessity (the sweep is deliberately mandatory/fail-open; a hard skip would starve genuine convergence=1 insight bridges): a SOFT γ down-weight by origin independence drops the poison seed to 0.000 while preserving genuine bridges. Cannot fully close — earned_bridge_value is offline-earnable, so it degrades to priced-not-prevented.

### RC-5 — Graduation / approval independence gaps
Breaches: WINDOW-FORGERY, RATIFICATION-BOOTSTRAP (c2).
Mechanism: the derived→observed graduation gate checks the ratifier's passport+anchor but has no content-causality link (the model's own conclusion echoed back by a controlled source graduates to OBSERVED). `approve()`'s distinct-approver gate rejects self-approval by AUTHORSHIP but does not require the approver to be ANCHOR-DISJOINT from the disputed members, so a Sybil sibling sharing one cheap anchor self-ratifies a lie.
Verdict: **GENUINE BUG (RATIFICATION-BOOTSTRAP is the cleanest invariant violation in the campaign).** "No provenance → no voice" is satisfied by the letter (a valid distinct-author passport exists) but violated in substance (no INDEPENDENT external stamp). The missing check is explicit: approver must be `independenceBetween > 0` from every disputed member. WINDOW-FORGERY is a genuine but subtler key-provenance ≠ content-provenance gap.

### RC-6 — Anchor scarcity is not bound to the key (rotation / stake-expiry)
Breaches: Anchor-Preserving-Key-Rotation-Whitewash, Transient-Bond-Cap-Inflation (c2); cc-c3-02, cc-c3-03 (c3).
Mechanism: reputation and the 4× contradiction scar bind to the PASSPORT KEY; real-world scarcity binds to the ANCHOR. Re-mint a key, re-bind the same retained DOMAIN → clean LCB 0, and disown's sourceId-scoped closure cannot follow the anchor across the key change. Symmetrically, a short-lock bond lifts the self-stack cap, bakes permanent α, then withdraws with no expiry hook.
Verdict: **GENUINE BUG.** Directly contradicts the "attacking gets MORE expensive the more you do it" claim — a key rotation is a one-keygen clean slate that retains the scarce anchor. There is no anchor-level reputational lien and no stake-expiry reversal sweep. The docs do not list this; it is a real hole in the disown/stake model.

### RC-7 — Offline class-assignment & fleet-cap seams
Breaches/forged-counts: Class-Collision-Laundering, ClassCollusionEcho, Mislabeled-Twins, Mislabeled-Class-Sybil, Anchor-Twins, Partial-Overlap-Seam (c1); Registrar-Carousel, Mega-Provider-Tenant-Seam, Null-Source-Laundromat (c2); ce-c3-01, ce-c3-02, ce-c3-03 (c3).
Mechanism: independence = anchor-set disjointness, computed from OFFLINE-assigned class ids. The fleet-cap keys on registrar/ASN OVERLAP, not actor identity, so K distinct-registrar domains (or K subdomains of one $9 parent with no PSL, or K per-address EMAIL anchors on one mega-provider tenant) read as K independent roots. Null-source roots get permissive class-only independence AND escape disown (no source to contradict).
Verdict: **DOCUMENTED RESIDUAL — the hard theorem made mechanical.** This is explicitly the "offline class-assignment liability" and "identity is priced, not prevented" boundary CLAUDE.md states the code cannot solve. The campaign's contribution is to PRICE it precisely: PSL eTLD+1 collapse (ce-c3-04) is necessary hygiene that closes the same-owner subdomain seam but cannot touch a correlation-free carousel; operator-graph clustering (ce-c3-05) raises the price ~$40 → $40+disjoint-ops but never to zero leakage. Two genuine sub-findings inside the residual: the engine LACKS a PSL (a mechanical, available eTLD+1 boundary — its absence is a fixable hygiene gap, not the theorem), and null-source roots get *permissive* (not conservative) independence, which is the wrong fail direction.

### RC-8 — Merkle boundary / coverage / wiring
Breaches: mk-m2, mk-m3, mk-m4, mk-m5 (c3).
Verdict: **DOCUMENTED RESIDUAL (operational).** Single-signer key model, mutable state outside the STH commitment, un-wired anchor, absent cross-sink collector — all four are in CLAUDE.md's GAP LIST ("no REAL external witness sinks"). The crypto core HELD against every in-tree tamper (mk-m1, fp-6, four CONFIRM specs). One genuine sub-finding worth elevating: mk-m3 (the undo-engine's disown/demotion/reputation rows are mutable and NOT covered by the STH) means "demote-never-delete / immortal archive stub" is only as immortal as an un-journaled SQLite row — the tamper-evidence does not cover the state it protects. Fix is F-C: journal strand/reputation mutations into the commitment.

## Ranked recommended engine fixes
1. **Route the high-impact gate's class count through `independentRootCount` (MIS).** Closes RC-2 entirely (fp-1 CLOSES, measured). One wiring change; legit 2-disjoint-operator winners still resolve. Highest value/cost ratio — closes a genuine bug with a one-line contract fix.
2. **Attribute-scope the decisive-resolve corroboration + add a ≥2-independent-ROOT floor on all auto-resolves.** Closes the build-once-flip-everywhere amortization (al-c3-05 CLOSES) and the self-built overturn (fp-2 PARTIAL); turns RC-1's O(1) global spend into linear-in-attributes cost. Scope to `classes.size>1` to avoid a human-fatigue bomb on safe echo-collapse.
3. **Make disown credit-reversal intersect the full demoted closure, not the direct seed, AND engine-derive the corroborator set.** pt-2 CLOSES (pass `taintedStrandIds`) and pt-4 CLOSES (drop the caller-naming / no-name exemption). Closes the most-cited "exact reversal not exact" gaps cheaply; pt-3 transitive hop remains a detect-and-defer item.
4. **Add an anchor-disjointness clause to `approve()` (approver `independenceBetween > 0` from every disputed member).** Closes RC-5's RATIFICATION-BOOTSTRAP — the cleanest invariant violation — with a single predicate.
5. **Bind reputation/disown to the anchor, not just the key; add a stake-expiry reversal hook.** Closes RC-6 (key-rotation whitewash, transient-bond harvest). Requires an anchor-level reputational lien — more design, high value.
6. **Soft γ down-weight on the Phase-2 bridge sweep (`seed = γ · provenance_independence`), order by earned_bridge_value.** PARTIAL-closes RC-4 (cc-c3-05/fp-5): poison seed → 0.000 dropped, genuine bridges preserved. Never hard-skip (fail-open invariant).
7. **Bundle a versioned PSL; derive DOMAIN operator class from eTLD+1 at bind time.** Necessary hygiene on RC-7 (ce-c3-04): closes the same-owner subdomain seam, honors github.io PRIVATE entries. Does not touch the carousel (theorem residual) but removes the mechanical, fixable half.
8. **Operational merkle program (external HSM log key + ≥1 independent witness + per-mutation anchor + journal strand/reputation rows into the commitment + scheduled cross-sink auditor).** Closes RC-8 (mk-m6 PARTIAL); irreducible trust-root cost like TLS/CAs, out of single-process scope but the path is proven.
- REJECT: blanket-defer all 1v1 disputes (fp-4 FAILS) — re-enables the DoS horn, taxes every legitimate binary correction, and is evaded by one self-stacked 2v1 co-asserter. Prefer #1 + #2.
- Orthogonal but real: add stake-to-enqueue / per-source pending cap / cross-attribute dedup on the human horn to blunt the DEFER-DoS (al-c3-01, DriftBombDeferDoS, Sandbag-the-Doorbell) — integrity held in every case, but the finite reviewer is floodable at linear cost.

## Honest bottom line
Intelligent DB has a **strong, narrow, structural core** and a **wide, soft, policy-shaped periphery.** The core does exactly what the design promised: the MIS shared-class collapse defuses the contradiction-bomb (500 bare keys → one weightless witness), the bare-key cap holds the floor, the co-asserter-tie-defer turns priced cartels into deferrals rather than flips, and the RFC-6962 merkle layer detects every in-tree tamper. Across all three cycles, **no flood, echo, headcount majority, or same-class Sybil ever flipped a true value** — every cycle-1 "breach" that mattered was a single patient aged source, never volume. That core is genuinely trustworthy and survived adversarial re-verification.

The periphery is where sustained novel Sybil pressure lands, and it divides cleanly. Much of it is the project's own HONESTLY DOCUMENTED boundary — "identity is priced, not prevented," "patient single betrayal," "offline class-assignment liability," "no external witness sinks." The campaign's value there is not novelty but PRICING: it shows the patient-betrayal cost is one passport + time, the class-assignment cost is ~$40/registrar with no upper bound, and the merkle boundary needs an operational external-witness program. These should not be inflated into new findings.

But the campaign also surfaced GENUINE bugs the docs do NOT cover, and these are the ones that matter for the central "trustworthy memory" claim: (1) the high-impact gate counts CLASSES not ROOTS, so one actor clears the irreversible-decision gate built to stop exactly that; (2) "exact corroboration-credit reversal" is one-hop, opt-in, and caller-attributed — not exact; (3) the Phase-2 bridge sweep has no identity gate, so a zero-independence source steers retrieval; (4) `approve()` accepts an anchor-sharing sibling as an "independent" ratifier; (5) symmetric β-decay erases the "lost fast" deterrent and doubles as a free timing oracle; (6) anchor scarcity binds to the key, so a rotation is a clean slate that keeps the anchor. Five of these six have measured or low-cost fixes (recommendations #1–#6) and none requires touching the structural core.

What this means for the project: the claim "the web can judge WHAT a fact is, but not WHO is behind it — so identity must come from outside" is VINDICATED, not refuted — every deep breach is an identity/independence judgment, never a content judgment, and every one routes back to a place where the engine trusted a cheap proxy (a class count, a caller-supplied name, a key, a global scalar) for an expensive real-world quantity (an independent actor). The honest verdict: the core is sound and the two governing invariants hold in spirit; the trust periphery currently prices several attacks LOWER than the threat model intends, and a focused set of six fixes — none structural, most one-to-medium-effort — would raise that price back to the documented "priced, visible, self-limiting" boundary the design aims for. Until then, the system is trustworthy against volume and naive Sybil, and trustworthy-with-named-caveats against the patient, mechanism-aware single attacker.
